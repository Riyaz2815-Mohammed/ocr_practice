Types of customer Journey Maps:

1. Current State Map
represents the customer's present experience with the system.

2. Day-in-the-life map
shows the customer's daily activities, including interactions not directly related to the product or service.

3. Future state map:
Illustrates the desired or improved customer experience after solutions are implemented.

4. Service blueprint Map:
connects customer experience with internal processes, people and technology.

How to create a customer Journey Map:

1. Identify the target customer or persona
2. Define the scenario or goal
3. List the journey stages (before, during, after)
4. Identify customer actions at each stage
5. Note customer thoughts and emotions
6. Identify pain points and challenges
7. Highlight opportunities for improvement
8. Represent the journey visually or in structured format
